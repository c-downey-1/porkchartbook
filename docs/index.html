Monthly Key Insights

Highlights from the latest public data, refreshed monthly.
  • The US breeding herd has eased to ~5.89M head, down from ~6.02M two years ago — a slow, productivity-driven contraction rather than a culling spike. View chart →
  • Pigs saved per litter has climbed to ~12.0, up from ~11.7 a year ago — more pigs from fewer sows is keeping output steady as the herd shrinks. View chart →
  • The packer spread (cutout less net hog price) has compressed to just a few dollars per cwt recently — unusually thin processing margins. View chart →
  • Exports now absorb ~26% of US pork production, with 2025 monthly volumes running above the prior-decade range. View chart →
  • Frozen pork stocks sit near 436M lb, ~4% below a year ago — leaner cold storage points to firm demand absorbing supply. View chart →
